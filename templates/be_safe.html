How to Be Safe Online ?

Quick, practical steps to protect your accounts, privacy, and devices.

1) Strong Passwords & Manager Basics

  • Use unique passwords for every account. Never reuse the same password.
  • Aim for 16+ characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid personal info (names, birthdays, sports teams).
  • Prefer a password manager to generate and store complex passwords.
  • Use a strong master password you can remember (passphrase style).
Examples
Weak 123456, password
OK Hesham2009!
Great nF8wL!e3pC#0tZq7R

2) Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Even if your password leaks, 2FA can block attackers by requiring a second proof (code/prompt).
  • Prefer authenticator apps or security keys over SMS when possible.
  • Save your backup codes in a safe place.
  • Turn on login alerts to be notified of suspicious sign-ins.

3) Spot Phishing & Scams

Red flags
  • Urgent tone: “Your account will be deleted today!”
  • Unexpected attachments or login links.
  • Misspellings, strange sender address, or look-alike domains.
  • Requests for passwords, codes, or payment cards.
What to do
  • Don’t click—visit the site directly from your bookmarks.
  • Verify with the company via official channels.
  • Use separate email addresses for important accounts.
  • Report phishing to the provider and delete it.

4) Secure Your Devices

  • Keep OS, browser, and apps up to date (auto-update on).
  • Use a device passcode or biometric unlock, and encrypt your disk if available.
  • Install apps from trusted stores only.
  • Review app permissions; remove what you don’t use.
  • Turn on Find My Device and remote wipe features.

5) Privacy Controls & Sharing

Share less. Anything public can be copied, scraped, and stored forever.
  • Lock down social profiles; limit who can see personal details.
  • Think before posting travel plans, addresses, school/work details.
  • Review ad/privacy settings; opt out of unnecessary tracking.
  • Use different emails for banking, shopping, and newsletters.

6) Safer Browsing & Wi-Fi

  • Prefer HTTPS websites; avoid entering passwords on HTTP pages.
  • Be careful on public Wi-Fi. Avoid sensitive logins on open networks.
  • Turn off auto-connect to open networks; forget networks you don’t need.
  • Use a modern browser with phishing/malware protection enabled.

7) Breaches, Backups & Recovery

  • If a site you use is breached: change the password immediately and enable 2FA.
  • Never reuse breached passwords anywhere else.
  • Back up important files.
  • Store recovery email/phone up to date; keep backup codes safe.

8) Secure Habits Checklist

  • Unique password for every site
  • 2FA enabled where available
  • Software auto-updates on
  • Phishing awareness (verify links)
  • Privacy settings reviewed
  • Public Wi-Fi caution
  • Regular backups configured
  • Recovery options up to date
